2. Appointments and Cancellations

Therapy sessions are scheduled in advance and represent a reserved time exclusively for you.

  • Duration: Standard individual sessions are 50 minutes long. Couples therapy sessions (EFT) are typically 60 to 75 minutes, as agreed upon.
  • Punctuality: If you arrive late, the session will still end at the scheduled time to accommodate other clients.
  • Cancellation Policy: We require a minimum of 24 hours' notice for cancellations or rescheduling. Sessions canceled with less than 24 hours' notice, or missed appointments (no-shows), will be charged at the full session rate. Exceptions are made only for sudden illness or verifiable emergencies.

3. Fees and Payment

Transparency regarding fees is an important part of our professional relationship.

  • Current session fees are discussed and agreed upon prior to our first appointment.
  • Payment is due at the end of each session or in advance via bank transfer, depending on the agreed billing arrangement.
  • Invoices are issued in accordance with Romanian financial regulations.
  • We reserve the right to review and adjust fees periodically. Clients will be notified at least 30 days in advance of any fee changes.

4. The Nature of Psychological Services

Psychotherapy is a collaborative process that requires active effort on your part. While it often leads to significant benefits, such as improved relationships and resolution of specific problems, there are no guarantees of what you will experience. Therapy can involve discussing unpleasant aspects of your life, which may lead to temporary feelings of sadness, anxiety, or frustration.

By engaging in therapy, you acknowledge that progress depends on various factors, and you agree to participate openly and honestly in the process.

5. Emergency Situations

Our practice does not provide emergency or crisis intervention services outside of scheduled session times. We do not offer a 24-hour crisis line.

In the event of a mental health emergency, or if you feel you are at risk of harming yourself or others, please immediately:

  • Call the national emergency number: 112
  • Go to the nearest hospital emergency department.
  • Contact a dedicated crisis helpline available in your area.
